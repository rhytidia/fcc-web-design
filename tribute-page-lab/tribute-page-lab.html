The Amazing Crow

Fascinating things about crows!

  • Some research has shown crows alert others when they find a dead crow, which may be a way to learn about places or individuals who pose a danger (Audobon Society).
  • If you're near the ocean, you may see crows pick up shellfish, fly upwards a ways, then drop them on the ground to crack them open (personal experience). They may do the same with nuts (Audobon Society).
  • In some places there is a "crow highway" where large groups of crows fly together away from their roosting site to feeding grounds in the morning, and then back to the roosting site in the evening (CBC).
  • Research and anecdotal evidence has suggested that crows remember human faces, particularly if they have done something threatening or helpful for the crows. And other crows watching can learn these faces too! (Discover Magazine, CTV News)
  • Crows can not only use tools, one species has been shown in a research study to be able to put multiple pieces of things together to create "compound tools" when a single item isn't enough to solve a problem (Science Alert).

These are just some of the reall amazing things crows can do. If you want to dig deeper into crow behaviour, the Corvid Research Blog by Kaeli Swift has a lot of great information!
